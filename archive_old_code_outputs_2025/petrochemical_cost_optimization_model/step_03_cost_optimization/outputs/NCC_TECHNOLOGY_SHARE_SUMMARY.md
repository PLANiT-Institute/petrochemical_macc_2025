# NCC Technology Share Analysis - Summary Report

## Overview

This report analyzes the technology transition pathways for Naphtha Cracking Complexes (NCC) in Korea's petrochemical industry across three decarbonization scenarios (2025-2050).

**NCC Scope:** Includes Olefins and Aromatics production facilities (Ethylene, Propylene, Butadiene, Benzene, Toluene, Xylene, Styrene).

**Total NCC Baseline Emissions (2025):** ~31.2 MtCO2 (60% of total industry emissions)

---

## Technology Pathways

### Three Decarbonization Technologies for NCC:

1. **Fossil Fuel (Baseline)** - Traditional naphtha crackers
   - Emission factor: 0.0149 tCO2/GJ
   - Current technology, no additional costs
   - Continues until replaced by low-carbon alternatives

2. **NCC-H2 (Green Hydrogen)** - Hydrogen-fueled crackers
   - Replaces naphtha with green hydrogen
   - H2 price: $6.0/kg (2025) → $1.2/kg (2050)
   - MACC cost: $273/tCO2 (2025) → $73/tCO2 (2050)
   - Zero direct emissions

3. **NCC-Electricity (Electric Crackers)** - Electrified crackers
   - Replaces naphtha with renewable electricity
   - RE price: $58/MWh (2025) → $32/MWh (2050)
   - MACC cost: $324/tCO2 (2025) → $92/tCO2 (2050)
   - Zero direct emissions (with renewable electricity)

## Cross-Scenario Analysis

### Technology Deployment Patterns:

1. **Timing:** All scenarios delay NCC conversion until 2035+
   - Heat pumps deployed first (cheaper, negative cost)
   - NCC conversion begins only after heat pump potential exhausted

2. **Technology Choice:** H2 preferred over electricity for all scenarios
   - NCC-H2 consistently cheaper than NCC-Electricity ($73 vs $92/tCO2 in 2050)
   - Electric crackers never economically competitive under these assumptions

3. **Deployment Scale:**
   - Peak deployment: 24-27% in 2040 across all scenarios
   - 2050 deployment: 10-16% depending on emission target stringency
   - Majority of NCC capacity remains fossil fuel-based through 2050

4. **Cost-Effectiveness:**
   - H2 becomes economically attractive only after 2035 due to:
     - Declining H2 prices ($6.0 → $1.2/kg)
     - Rising carbon prices ($50/tCO2 growing at 5%/year)
     - Technology maturity (available from 2030)

### Sensitivity to Emission Targets:

| Scenario | 2050 Target | H2 Share (2050) | Fossil Share (2050) |
|----------|-------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Point Targets | 5 MtCO2 | 10.2% | 89.8% |
| Linear | 2 MtCO2 | 16.1% | 83.9% |
| Budget | 2.5 MtCO2 | 15.0% | 85.0% |

**Insight:** More stringent 2050 targets drive higher H2 conversion rates, but the relationship is not linear due to:
- Other abatement options (heat pumps) deployed first
- Cost optimization prioritizes cheapest options
- Point Targets scenario focuses on 2040 target (20 MtCO2), allowing relaxation by 2050

## Methodology Notes

### NCC Emission Calculation:
- Identified NCC facilities by product keywords: Ethylene, Propylene, Butadiene, Benzene, Toluene, Xylene, Styrene
- Total NCC baseline emissions: Sum of all matching facilities
- NCC share of total: ~60% (industry-typical proportion)

### Technology Share Calculation:
```
NCC Fossil Share (%) = 100 × (Baseline NCC - H2 Deployed - Electricity Deployed) / Baseline NCC
NCC H2 Share (%) = 100 × H2 Deployed / Baseline NCC
NCC Electricity Share (%) = 100 × Electricity Deployed / Baseline NCC
```

### Limitations:
1. Assumes homogeneous NCC facilities (actual capacities vary)
2. Does not account for facility-specific constraints (location, age, etc.)
3. H2 and electricity prices follow trajectories (actual prices may vary)
4. No consideration of hybrid technologies (partial H2 blending, etc.)

## Conclusion

**Key Takeaway:** Green hydrogen emerges as the primary decarbonization pathway for Korean NCC facilities starting in the mid-2030s, with 10-16% of capacity expected to convert by 2050 under current cost assumptions and policy scenarios.

**Critical Success Factors:**
1. H2 price decline trajectory ($6 → $1.2/kg)
2. H2 infrastructure development (pipelines, storage, supply)
3. Technology readiness and commercial availability (2030+)
4. Carbon price incentives (rising from $50/tCO2)

**Strategic Priority:** Develop integrated H2 supply infrastructure in major petrochemical clusters (Yeosu, Ulsan, Daesan) to enable cost-effective conversion of 15-20% of NCC capacity by 2050.
