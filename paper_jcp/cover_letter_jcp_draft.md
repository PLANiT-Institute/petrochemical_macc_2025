Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript, "Techno-Economic Analysis of Deep Decarbonization Pathways for the South Korean Petrochemical Industry," for consideration as an Original Research article in the Journal of Cleaner Production.

This manuscript presents a facility-level transition pathway analysis for one of Asia's largest petrochemical hubs under carbon-budget constraints from 2025 to 2050. The central contribution is not a MACC exercise itself, but a decision-oriented transition strategy that quantifies the timing, scale, and policy conditions required to deliver net-zero pathways in a hard-to-abate sector. In our framework, marginal abatement cost metrics are used as diagnostic indicators to support prioritization, while the research objective is to identify feasible transition pathways and policy design implications.

Key findings include:
- Early annual investment needs on the order of USD 7.3 billion by 2030 under the high-ambition pathway.
- Convergence of annual transition costs near USD 25 billion by 2050 with approximately 51.5 MtCO2 abatement.
- Distinct technology sequencing across time, with strong early electrification signals and later hydrogen dependence under infrastructure constraints.
- Policy-relevant evidence for combining upfront CAPEX support with long-horizon operating-risk instruments (e.g., CCfD-like mechanisms).

We believe this work fits JCP's scope because it integrates cleaner production strategy, industrial decarbonization planning, and actionable policy design with transparent, reproducible modeling.

This manuscript is original, has not been published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission and have no conflicts of interest to declare, except as disclosed in the manuscript.
