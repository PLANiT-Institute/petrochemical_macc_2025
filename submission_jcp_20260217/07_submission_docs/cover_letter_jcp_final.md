Dear Editor,

I submit my manuscript, "Infrastructure Bottlenecks in Petrochemical Decarbonization: Facility-Level Evidence from South Korea (2025-2050)," as an Original Research article for Journal of Cleaner Production.

This study provides a facility-level transition analysis using a reproducible evidence workflow. The core contribution is a hypothesis test on infrastructure feasibility: whether pathway outcomes are constrained more by electricity and hydrogen infrastructure than by static cost ordering.

Key quantified findings include:
- In the high-ambition pathway, 2030 transition needs are 7.273 BUSD/yr for 1.902 MtCO2/yr abatement.
- In 2050, the same pathway reaches 25.043 BUSD/yr and 51.505 MtCO2/yr abatement.
- Electricity-centered pathways require about 146.034 TWh/yr added electricity demand by 2050.
- Hydrogen-centered pathways require about 4.546 Mt/yr hydrogen plus 32.380 TWh/yr added electricity by 2050.

I believe the manuscript fits JCP because it links cleaner production transition strategy to verifiable evidence and policy implementation constraints.

This manuscript is original, not under review elsewhere, and approved by the author. Data and verification artifacts are packaged for reproducibility.
